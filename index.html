Project Evaluation Tool

The Project Evaluation Tool aims to assist in assessing the ethicality of development projects based on the 7 values of worthwhile development: well-being, equality, empowerment, human rights, cultural freedom, responsibility, and sustainability. To utilize the Project Evaluation Tool for scoring a project, assess how well the project reflects each value of worthwhile development on a scale from 1-5. For each criterion, consider the level of attention and commitment, identifying any shortcomings or areas that require improvement. Assign scores accordingly, ensuring consistency in judgment across all criteria. After completing the individual assessments of each value, calculate the overall score by summing up the scores from each criterion and dividing by the total number of criteria. This final score will categorize the project as Inadequate, Developing, Adequate, Exemplary, or Exceptional, providing a comprehensive and nuanced evaluation of its ethicality across various dimensions

Well-being: The project fails to address the well-being of affected communities or negatively impacts communities. Overall satisfaction of affected communities and stakeholders is low. (1)

Equality: The study neglects to foster equality within its implementation or worsens existing inequalities. (1)

Empowerment: The study fails to promote the self-sufficiency and autonomy of affected communities. There is little to no sense of agency or self-determination among stakeholders. (1)

Human Rights: The project neglects or directly violates human rights principles throughout the project lifecycle. There are no protections in place to mitigate any potential risks to human rights. (1)

Cultural Freedom: The project neglects or dismisses cultural freedom, with little regard for the preservation of cultural values.(1)

Responsibility: The project neglects or dismisses responsibility, with little consideration for ethical guidelines and accountability. (1)

Sustainability: The project lacks any meaningful sustainability measures and may harm future prospects. (1)

Well-being: The impact on well-being is limited, with several shortcomings in addressing the relational aspects and satisfaction of participants. (2)

Equality: Limited focus on equality, with significant disparities or discriminatory practices that require attention. (2)

Empowerment: Limited impact on empowerment, with insufficient support for participant agency and self-determination. (2)

Human Rights: Limited commitment to human rights, with substantial concerns and violations that require immediate correction. (2)

Cultural Freedom: Limited focus on cultural freedom, with potential disregard for cultural values and practices. (2)

Responsibility: Limited commitment to responsibility, with substantial concerns in ethical practices that require attention. (2)

Sustainability: Limited consideration for sustainability, with potential negative long-term impacts that need attention. (2)

Well-being: The project has a moderate impact on well-being, but there are areas where improvements could enhance the overall experience for participants. (3)

Equality: Moderate attention is given to equality, but there are noticeable gaps or instances of bias that need to be addressed. (3)

Empowerment: Moderate empowerment is achieved, but additional measures are needed to further support participant agency. (3)

Human Rights: Moderate adherence to human rights, with noticeable gaps or oversights that need attention. (3)

Cultural Freedom: Moderate attention is given to cultural freedom, but there are noticeable gaps or instances where cultural values are not fully considered. (3)

Responsibility: Moderate attention is given to responsibility, but there are noticeable gaps or instances where ethical considerations need refinement. (3)

Sustainability: Moderate sustainability practices are in place, with room for improvement in certain aspects. (3)

Well-being: The project significantly contributes to well-being, with notable positive effects on relationships and the quality of life for participants. (4)

Equality: Considerable efforts are made to promote equality, with some areas for refinement in addressing disparities. (4)

Empowerment: Significant efforts are made to empower participants, with some areas for improvement to enhance overall agency. (4)

Human Rights: Significant attention is given to human rights, with minor areas for improvement in ensuring adherence to principles. (4)

Cultural Freedom: Considerable efforts are made to respect and incorporate cultural freedom, with some areas for further enhancement. (4)

Responsibility: Considerable efforts are made to uphold responsibility, with minor areas for improvement in ethical considerations. (4)

Sustainability: There is a strong emphasis on sustainability, with some areas for enhancement in addressing long-term impacts. (4)

Well-being: The project demonstrates a profound and measurable positive impact on the well-being of individuals or communities involved, fostering strong relationships and overall satisfaction. (5)

Equality: The project demonstrates a strong commitment to equality, actively addressing and reducing disparities, and ensuring fair participation and access to benefits. (5)

Empowerment: The project effectively empowers individuals or communities, promoting self-sufficiency, autonomy, and significant personal and collective growth. (5)

Human Rights: The project consistently upholds human rights, with robust safeguards and proactive measures to prevent infringements. (5)

Cultural Freedom: The project actively promotes and respects cultural freedom, ensuring that cultural values are considered and preserved. (5)

Responsibility: The project demonstrates a high level of responsibility in its actions, considering the ethical implications and accountability to stakeholders. (5)

Sustainability: The project is highly sustainable, considering environmental, economic, and social aspects for long-term benefit. (5)
